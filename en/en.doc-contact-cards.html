Contact Cards

Introduction

Contact cards make it easy for you to manage your contacts by associating incoming and outgoing interactions with specific people. This helps keep track of them in the future.

Note:

The contact card history keeps track of all previous interactions and links them to specific contacts.

Each contact that you interact with has the following set of properties associated with the group they are assigned to:

Property Icon Description

First name

Indicates the first name of a contact.

Last name

Indicates the last name of a contact.

Phone

Indicates the phone number(s) of a contact.

Email

Indicates the email address(es) of a contact.

Contact ID

Indicates the ID of a contact.

Note:

Your user interface automatically creates and assigns an ID to each contact, and this ID cannot be edited.

Using Contact Cards

Creating a New Contact ...

In your user interface, you have two options to create a new contact. You can either create them from scratch, or from an ongoing interaction.

... From Scratch

To create a new contact from scratch, follow these steps:

Step Action

1.

In the left menu of your user interface, click Contacts > + Add contact.

A new Unknown contact card appears.

2.

Fill in the Unknown contact card as follows:

  • In the First name field, enter the first name of the contact.

    Caution:

    This First name field is mandatory and its content must be at least 3 characters long.

    Example:
    John

  • In the Last name field, enter the last name of the contact.

    Example:
    Doe

  • In the Phone number field, enter the phone number of the contact.

    Caution:

    Make sure you start this phone number by adding a + symbol followed by the necesary calling code (e.g., +33).

    To find the comprehensive list of calling codes, refer to the Wikipedia documentation on country calling codes.

    Example:
    +33765435676

    Note:

    You can associate several phone numbers with a specific contact. To do so:

    1. Enter the first phone number in the Phone number field.

    2. Click the icon.

      A new field appears under the Phone number field.

    3. Fill in this new field with the desired phone number.

      Example:
      +33467890775

    4. Repeat step 2 and step 3 as many times as necesary.

  • In the Email address field, enter the email address of the contact.

    Example:
    john.doe@dbc.com

    Note:

    You can associate several email addresses with a specific contact. To do so:

    1. Enter the first email address in the Email address field.

    2. Add a semicolon ; character and a whitespace character after the first email address.

    3. Enter an additional email address in the Email address field.

      Example:
      john.doe@dbc.com; louis.dupont@dbc.com

    4. Repeat step 2 and step 3 as many times as necesary.

3.

Click the Save button to complete the creation of a new contact.

Congratulations! You have created a new contact.

... From an ongoing interaction

In your user interface, when you have an ongoing interaction, the platform automatically indicates who you are interacting with.

Note:

When this interaction is with an existing contact:

  • their contact card appears,

  • their personal details are displayed (e.g., first name, last name, etc.), and

  • the interaction is automatically assigned to this contact card.

When this interaction is with an unknown contact, an Unknown contact card appears with the Phone number field filled with the unknown contact's phone number.

Example:
+33765435676

Note:

If the phone number of this unknown contact has been used in campaigns, you can select a campaign ID if you want to process this call as an outgoing call and display the corresponding call script.

For more information, see the documentation on Campaigns.

Note:

During an ongoing interaction, you can find out if you have previously interacted with an unknown contact. To do so, unfold the Most recent interactions menu.

In addition, in case the user interface finds a connection between the phone number of an unkown contact and the email address of an existing contact (e.g., in the email signature of an email you have received), it displays the email or email thread under the Most recent interactions menu. This feature allows you to easily find and continue an ongoing email thread.

For more information, see the documentation on Interactions.

Searching for a Contact

To search for a contact, follow these steps:

Step Action

1.

In the left menu of your user interface, click Contacts .

The Contacts page appears.

2.

In the Contacts search bar, search for a contact.

Note:

If you have filled in someone's personal information before, you can find their contact details by searching using their first or last name, their email address, or their phone number.

The contact appears in the search results.

Editing a Contact

To edit a contact, follow these steps:

Step Action

1.

In the left menu of your user interface, click Contacts .

The Contacts page appears.

2.

In the Contacts search bar, search for the contact to edit.

Note:

If you have filled in someone's personal information before, you can find their contact details by searching using their first or last name, their email address, or their phone number.

The contact appears in the search results.

3.

Select the contact.

A right menu displaying the name of the contact appears on the right side of your user interface.

4.

In the right menu displaying the full name of the contact, click the More actions button.

5.

Click Edit.

The contact's associated contact card appears.

6.

Edit the contact as desired.

7.

Click the Save button to update the contact.

Congratulations! You have edited and updated the contact.

Deleting a Contact

To delete a contact, follow these steps:

Step Action

1.

In the left menu of your user interface, click Contacts .

The Contacts page appears.

2.

In the Contacts search bar, search for the contact to edit.

Note:

If you have filled in someone's personal information before, you can find their contact details by searching using their first or last name, their email address, or their phone number.

The contact appears in the search results.

3.

Select the contact.

A right menu displaying the name of the contact appears on the right side of your user interface.

4.

In the right menu displaying the full name of the contact, click the More actions button.

5.

Click Delete contact.

Warning:

Once you delete a contact, you cannot recover it. If you ever need it in the future, you will have to recreate it again from scratch.

A confirmation overlay appears.

6.

Click Yes delete to confirm the deletion of the contact.

Congratulations! You have deleted the contact.
